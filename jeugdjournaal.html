Bringing the Classroom Into Focus

Redesigning the Jeugdjournaal website to better serve a distinct and underserved user segment

Product Web
Skills User research, design sprint facilitation, interactive prototyping, UX/UI design, usability testing

Problem

The website launched at a time when desktop browsing was still finding its shape, and it was a solid response to that moment. A decade later, the context had quietly shifted. Digital whiteboards had made their way into classrooms across the Netherlands, and teachers were opening the site every morning to lead their class through the daily news. Around the same time, Jeugdjournaal had just launched its morning news concept, and early results were already showing that the layout needed to catch up with what they were learning. The strictly chronological structure pushed in-house productions down the page as soon as newer items went live, and the layout wasn't built for a room of thirty. It was always made for children, just never quite for this setting.

Research

To understand how teachers actually used the site, I distributed a survey to 128 teachers, parents, and caretakers, and followed it with in-depth interviews with 10 primary school teachers and 20 parents. The goal was to map real use cases: the daily routines, the workarounds, the friction, rather than rely on assumptions about what a classroom tool should do.

Findings

  • The chronological content structure buried in-house productions whenever news volume spiked. The most valuable content was the hardest to find.
  • Teachers visited every morning as part of a news ritual, but the layout gave them no reliable way to orient the session. Content could surface unexpectedly and derail the room.
  • Video-based articles were in high demand for quieter moments like lunch breaks, but there was no way to filter for them.
  • Classrooms read and discussed articles together, creating a shared experience that the individual-reader interface didn't support.

Solution

  • Restructured the homepage around the two daily moments that mattered most: morning and evening news
  • Introduced subject tagging, mirroring the playlist logic already familiar from Jeugdjournaal's YouTube channel
  • Added content-type labels so teachers could quickly identify and queue video articles
  • Gave prominent placement to in-house productions, which had previously competed poorly with high-frequency news content
  • Redesigned the poll as an interactive whiteboard element the whole room could participate in together
  • Moved reader comments alongside the article rather than below, so they could be read without interrupting video playback

Areas We'd Like to Explore

  • Making the morning and evening news segments more interactive, moving beyond simply embedding the broadcast
  • Creating space for short-form content that balances heavier topics
  • Enriching search to surface contextual depth rather than just a list of results
