Where Teenagers Come to Understand the World

Rebuilding a trusted news platform that meets teenagers where they are: in the classroom, on their terms, and at their own pace

Product Web
Skills User research, brand cocreation facilitation, interactive prototyping, UX/UI design, usability testing

Problem

NOS Stories had built something genuinely useful: a news format for teenagers structured around what is happening, why it matters, and how it resolves. But as the editorial team's responsibilities grew without the headcount to match, the website gradually fell away. For nearly two years it went without updates, while the team focused on short-form social media instead. By the time the project started, both editors and teenagers had little reason to open it.

The context was also shifting. The Netherlands had just introduced a law banning mobile phones in secondary schools, which reduced reliance on social media as a content channel overnight. Chromebooks, already used for schoolwork, became the primary screen for students during the day. The case for a web platform had quietly become much stronger.

Findings

  • All content lived on external platforms, creating a real risk. If an account were hacked or taken down, years of work could be permanently lost.
  • Social media algorithms distributed content broadly but unevenly. Editors published balanced coverage across multiple videos, but individual users only ever saw a portion of it, giving teenagers an incomplete picture.
  • Teachers regularly looked for video clips to enrich their lessons but had no efficient way to find relevant material beyond manually searching Instagram or YouTube.
  • Each year the team produces content to help final-year students prepare for their exams, streamed live on YouTube. This had made NOS Stories a uniquely trusted source across a student's entire secondary school journey, a relationship worth building on.

Solution

  • Created multiple content discovery modes: a chronological grid, a timeline view, and a structured planning view, making it easy to find current news, informational videos, and exam preparation content
  • Designed a video-first experience with web-native navigation suited to both short-form and long-form content
  • Developed a modular component system including a video player that adapts from horizontal to vertical across mobile and classroom whiteboards, enabling faster and more consistent development
  • Built a scalable content foundation designed to support future AI integration, enabling smarter search for both students and teachers

Areas We'd Like to Explore

  • Research shows teenagers feel increasingly fatigued by the news while still recognising the importance of staying informed. We want to experiment with a weekly format that is accessible and structured, giving teenagers tools to process and make sense of complex or ongoing topics.
  • As algorithmically driven content continues to erode media literacy, the platform has the potential to serve as a practical educational companion, allowing students to revisit and reflect on what they have learned long after a classroom session.
