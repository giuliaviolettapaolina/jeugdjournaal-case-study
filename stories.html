Giulia de Goede

Where Teenagers Come to Understand the World

Rebuilding a trusted news platform that meets teenagers where they are; in the classroom, on their terms, and at their own pace

Product Web
Skills User research, brand cocreation facilitation, interactive prototyping, UX/UI design, usability testing

Problem

The website had a strong editorial strategy for making news accessible to teenagers — articles opened with a bullet-point summary and were structured around a clear framework of what is happening, why it is happening, and how it resolves. However, as the editorial team didn't grow but their tasks substantially did, maintaining the website became unsustainable. The team shifted its focus toward short-form social media content, leaving the website without updates for nearly two years.

Both the editors and their teenage audience had little motivation to invest in the website, as it felt outdated and out of touch with how teenagers consumed media.

Findings

  • The team's heavy reliance on social media meant that all content lived on external platforms, creating a significant risk — if an account were hacked or taken down, years of content could be permanently lost
  • Although social media algorithms distributed content broadly, they created a fragmented and potentially biased experience. Editors would publish balanced coverage across multiple videos, but the algorithm would serve only selected pieces to individual users — giving teenagers an incomplete picture of the news
  • At the start of the project, the Netherlands introduced a law banning mobile phones in secondary schools. This significantly reduced reliance on social media as a content channel, while Chromebooks — already used for schoolwork — became the primary digital access point for students
  • Teachers regularly looked for video clips to enrich their lessons and needed a more efficient way to find relevant material than manually searching social media platforms such as Instagram or YouTube
  • Each year, the team produces content specifically designed to help final-year students prepare for their end-of-year exams, live-streamed on YouTube. This has established the team as a uniquely trusted news source, with a relationship that deepens across a student's entire secondary school journey

Solution

  • Created multiple content discovery modes — a chronological grid, a timeline view, and a structured planning view — making it easy to find current news, informational videos, and exam preparation content
  • Designed a video-first website experience with intuitive web-native navigation patterns suited to both short-form and long-form video content
  • Developed a flexible, modular component system — including a video player that adapts seamlessly from horizontal to vertical across mobile and classroom digital whiteboards — enabling faster, more consistent development
  • Established a scalable content foundation designed to support the future integration of AI, enabling smarter and more relevant search experiences for both students and teachers

Areas We'd Like to Explore

  • Research indicates that teenagers feel increasingly fatigued by the news, yet still recognise the importance of staying informed. Many struggle to disengage from social media and find it difficult to distinguish credible journalism from influencer content. There is a growing scepticism toward the press, with concerns about hidden agendas and a genuine appetite for neutral, fact-based reporting. We want to experiment with a weekly news format that is accessible, engaging, and empowering — giving teenagers the tools to process and make sense of complex or ongoing topics
  • As AI and algorithmically driven content continue to erode media and news literacy, the website has the potential to serve as a practical educational tool. Journalists already visit secondary schools weekly to discuss the role of a free press in the Netherlands — the platform could act as a companion resource for those sessions, allowing students to revisit and reflect on what they have learned long after the visit has taken place
